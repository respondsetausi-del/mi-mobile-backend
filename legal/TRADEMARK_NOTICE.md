### 7.1 Trademark Infringement

Trademark infringement occurs when:

- Someone uses a mark that is identical or confusingly similar to our trademarks
- The use is in connection with goods or services related to ours
- The use is likely to cause confusion, mistake, or deception among consumers
- The use dilutes or tarnishes our brand

### 7.2 Consequences of Infringement

Unauthorized use of our trademarks may result in:

- **Cease and Desist Notices:** Demand to stop infringing use immediately
- **Civil Litigation:** Lawsuits for trademark infringement
- **Injunctive Relief:** Court orders to stop infringing activities
- **Monetary Damages:** Compensation for actual damages and lost profits
- **Statutory Damages:** Up to $2,000,000 per counterfeit mark per type of goods or services (U.S. law)
- **Treble Damages:** Triple damages for willful infringement
- **Attorney's Fees:** Recovery of legal costs and attorney's fees
- **Destruction Orders:** Court-ordered destruction of infringing materials
- **Criminal Penalties:** In cases of counterfeiting (fines and imprisonment)

### 7.3 We Take Infringement Seriously
